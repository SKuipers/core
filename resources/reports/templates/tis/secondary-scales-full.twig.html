Performance Indicators
 7 A grade of 7 represents the level of ‘scholars’. This grade is reserved for students who have produced work of excellence level in all areas of assessment. Students with a grade 7 have consistently achieved outstanding results, have clearly met all course outcomes, produced work that illustrates higher level thinking skills and can apply their learning in both conventional and unconventional situations.
 6 A grade of 6 represents the level of ‘distinction’. This grade indicates that the student has consistently demonstrated a thorough understanding of the knowledge and skills defined in the course outcomes, is able to apply them effectively in a wide variety of situations and regularly produces work illustrating the skills of synthesis and evaluation. The student will have demonstrated originality, understanding and insight and produced quality work consistently in all areas of assessment.
 5 A grade of 5 represents the level of ‘honors’. This grade indicates that the student has regularly demonstrated a good understanding of the knowledge and skills defined in the course outcomes, is able to apply them effectively beyond conventional situations and, more often than not, produces work illustrating the skills of synthesis and evaluation. The student may occasionally, though not consistently, produce work showing originality and insight.
 4 A grade of 4 indicates that the student has demonstrated a satisfactory understanding and acquisition of the knowledge and skills defined in the course outcomes, and is able to apply them effectively in conventional situations. There is occasional evidence of the skills of synthesis and evaluation.
 3 A grade of 3 indicates some achievement in several areas of assessment. The student has demonstrated a basic understanding of the knowledge and skills defined in the course outcomes and is able to apply most of them in conventional situations.
 2 A grade of 2 indicates some, but limited achievement in most areas of assessment. The student has difficulty in understanding the knowledge and skills demanded by the course. The student has attempted most of the course objectives and has had some success in meeting course outcomes.
 1 A grade of 1 indicates minimal achievement by the student in terms of meeting the learning outcomes identified within the course.
Modified The student is participating in a 'modified' programme in this subject area with individualised outcomes and expectations.
 
Effort Indicators
 E Excellent
The student always shows a conscientious approach to his/her learning. Homework has been regularly completed, classwork has reflected consistent effort and all deadlines for submitted work have been met. The student actively participates in all class activities and is often self-managing in his/her learning journey.
 VG Very Good
The student has almost always shown a conscientious approach to his/her learning. Homework has been completed, classwork has almost always reflected consistent effort and deadlines for submitted work have almost always been met. The student actively participates in all class activities and is becoming self-managing in his/her learning journey.
 G Good
The student has usually shown a conscientious approach to his/her learning. Homework has usually been completed, classwork has usually reflected consistent effort and deadlines for submitted work have usually been met. The student participates in class but should seek to become more engaged in class activities and more self-managing in his/her learning journey.
 S Satisfactory
The student has shown a satisfactory approach to his/her learning, but engagement in the learning process has not been maximized. In order to achieve improved learning success, more consistency in completing coursework and more active participation in class activities would be beneficial.
 N Needs Improvement
The student’s effort toward learning has been unsatisfactory and a great improvement is required if the student is to make successful progress. Limited engagement in learning activities has restricted the student from reaching their full potential. A parent/teacher/student conference is highly recommended.
